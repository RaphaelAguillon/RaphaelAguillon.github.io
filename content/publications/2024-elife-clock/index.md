abstract: |
  We trace the evolutionary emergence of the circadian gene CLOCK in cnidarians and show
  that it already acted as a core regulator synchronizing internal molecular rhythms with diel
  environmental light cues. Combining transcriptional profiling, functional perturbations, and
  behavioral analysis, we demonstrate that cnidarian CLOCK integrates environmental timing
  with cellular physiology.
